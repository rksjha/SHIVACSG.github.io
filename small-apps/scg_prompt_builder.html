1

Copy the generated prompt

Click the Copy button in the top-right of the Prompt tab. This is your master instruction set for Claude.

2

Paste into Claude as a System Prompt

In Claude.ai → Settings → Custom Instructions, paste this as your default persona. Or in your Make/n8n HTTP module, pass it as the system field in the API call body.

3

Trigger daily with a simple user message

Every morning at 7 AM, your Make automation sends Claude just one line: "Generate today's social media posts." — the system prompt handles everything else.

4

Feed Claude a trending topic (optional)

For maximum relevance, have Make first call Perplexity AI or a Google News RSS feed, extract the top headline in your domain, then pass it to Claude as: "Today's news hook: [headline]. Generate posts."

5

Auto-send to Publer or Buffer

Connect the Claude output to Publer's API. Make will parse Claude's 3 post variants and schedule them for optimal times: 7:30 AM, 12:30 PM, and 6:30 PM IST.

6

Weekly review — 20 minutes only

Every Sunday, review the week's queue in Publer. Approve, tweak, or swap. The system runs itself; you just stay in editorial control.

⚡ Pro tip: Generate separate prompts for each day of the week using different configurations, then load the right one in your automation based on the day. This ensures Monday's MSME content is perfectly tuned differently from Friday's tender alerts.
